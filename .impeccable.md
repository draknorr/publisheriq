## Design Context

### Users
PublisherIQ serves a balanced mix of publishers, developers, investors, and market analysts working in professional Steam research and monitoring workflows. They use the product to evaluate games and companies, track change intelligence, compare entities, and answer business questions quickly across a large and complex catalog. The interface should support repeated daily use, dense analysis, and fast movement from question to evidence.

### Brand Personality
PublisherIQ should feel premium, editorial, and powerful. The tone should project confidence, control, and analytical authority while still feeling fast and modern. The product should help users feel both in command and efficient, not playful, hype-driven, or consumer-social.

### Aesthetic Direction
Keep the existing warm stone + dusty coral system as the foundation, with DM Sans for UI text and JetBrains Mono for numeric and data display. Treat both light and dark themes as first-class experiences. Preserve information density, strong hierarchy, data-forward presentation, and purposeful motion. Avoid neon gamer UI most strongly; also avoid overly blank minimalism and generic cold-blue SaaS styling.

### Design Principles
1. Design for expert multi-role analysis. Optimize for publishers, developers, investors, and analysts equally, with dense information that remains easy to scan.
2. Project confidence through warmth and precision. Use editorial composition, calm surfaces, and exact data typography instead of loud novelty.
3. Make powerful workflows feel immediate. Favor quick navigation, keyboard-friendly patterns, obvious state changes, and compact controls that feel fast without feeling frantic.
4. Maintain first-class accessibility across themes. Both light and dark modes should hold up at WCAG AA, with reduced-motion-friendly behavior and clear focus, hover, and selection states.
5. Stay distinctive without cliches. PublisherIQ should feel specific to high-end game-market intelligence, not like a generic SaaS admin and not like a neon gaming dashboard.
