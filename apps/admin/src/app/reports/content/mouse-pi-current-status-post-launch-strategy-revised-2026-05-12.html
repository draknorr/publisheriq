01 / Highest impact

What should happen next

Players have surfaced clear pain points that require a major quality-of-life update. The next beat should read as a response to that feedback, not a changelog. DLC is a separate, later message.

The recommendations below pull from PublisherIQ's view of player friction, support cadence, store positioning, and the competitive calendar.

High confidence Directional signal Strategic inference
01

Treat the next major patch as the reset moment.

Player feedback clusters on four areas: controls, quest handling, bugs, and the late-game cleanup path. The patch should answer all four. The framing players need to hear is straightforward: feedback registered, sharp edges sanded, a reason to reopen the case.

Highest impact
02

Keep support and DLC on different beats.

Public trust signals are still mixed and future-content details are ambiguous. Until both stabilize, the near-term message has to be confidence — what's fixed, what's coming next, and when. Deluxe and future content can stay visible on the page, but that's a different audience job from selling DLC.

02 / Current read

Where MOUSE stands now

Launch attention is still alive. Players are posting about the premise and listing fixes they want. The hard part is the bridge from that opening into sustained play.

High confidence
Product strength

Style, noir mood, jazz score, and distinctiveness in the FPS field.

Positive public language clusters on the same four elements: the black-and-white cartoon style, the noir mood, the jazz score, and the fact that nothing else in the current FPS field reads like it.

High confidence
Product weakness

Feel, bugs, progression clarity, and perceived value pull negative once the visuals stop carrying.

Player-response language and progression behavior both show the same shift: once the visuals stop carrying the experience on their own, four questions get louder — feel, bugs, progression clarity, and whether the price feels earned.

High confidence
Commercial implication

The highest-leverage moves are about order. The next public beat is a substantive patch, the store page refreshes to match what's resonating, and DLC arrives only after post-launch responsiveness has been visible long enough to carry the message.

05 / Store page

Optimization options

Two jobs for the store page: clarify the promise, and make the support story credible. The page has the words it needs already — it's the order of proof that's off.

High confidence Strategic inference
Area Recommended change Why it matters
First-screen promise Open with "1930s cartoon noir FPS" and a concrete action promise — before any broader genre framing. The identity is what differentiates the game. Retro-shooter framing drops MOUSE into a crowded comparison set.
Trailer and screenshot order Order the cut so style lands first, then combat feel, then the detective hook, then mission variety. Players already see the visual hook. The job now is showing the game underneath sustains it.
Post-launch support block Refresh the copy around the major patch — controls, bug cleanup, quest handling, progression. Lets the support work double as purchase reassurance.
Deluxe / DLC clarity Say what Deluxe includes and what "future story DLC" actually covers, and when the next timing update lands. Keeps the ambiguity from turning into a trust issue.
Review and quote selection Pull quotes that pair identity with playability. Praise for the look alone leaves the gameplay question open. The conversion gap is the playability question. The look is already doing its work; the game underneath needs the same proof.

06 / Timing

Post-launch beat sequence

Three public beats, in order: patch, reactivation, DLC roadmap. They shouldn't collapse into one message.

Directional signal Strategic inference
May 18-20

Primary patch window

Ship the substantive patch — controls, bugs, quest handling, late-game cleanup. The window holds before the calendar gets noisier.

Jun 2-4

Backup patch window

Hold this slot only if the patch isn't ready in May. Same message hierarchy — and don't let the update read as a changelog.

Jun 15-17

Reactivation beat

Give players who finished the case a small reason to come back — tracked cleanup, a clearer collectible loop, or a community challenge.

Aug 10-11

DLC roadmap window

Spell out what's included, roughly when, and where the creative direction is heading. The tone here is reassurance.

Avoid the most crowded release weeks — lookalike FPS launches and attention-adjacent games will make a support or DLC message harder to land. Take the earliest patch slot only if the update genuinely includes the player-facing improvements named here.
