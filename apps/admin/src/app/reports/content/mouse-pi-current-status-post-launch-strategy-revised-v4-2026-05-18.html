MOUSE: P.I. For Hire

MOUSE is still selling on a rubberhose noir promise. The next commercial risk is not awareness. It is whether the players who bought the promise keep attaching a repair list to the recommendation.

Verdict

Jack Pepper has the hook. The cleanup path is where the recommendation breaks.

Current Steam API data shows 11,126 all-language reviews with 10,513 positive, roughly 94.5%. That score is healthy, but the language underneath is already splitting: players praise the black-and-white cartoon-noir fantasy, then qualify the buy recommendation around replay, cleanup, weapon depth, bugs, and value. A sale, showcase, or DLC push brings in a colder buyer cohort that judges MOUSE less on novelty and more on whether the $29.99 game is finished enough to recommend without a caveat.

Sources

Findings draw on the May 10 PublisherIQ archive (10,283 Steam reviews, 7,484 forum/comment records, 1,099 threads), the May 11 500-player progression sample, Steam Storefront/review API checks on May 18, official Steam announcements through Hotfix v1.0.6, and PublisherIQ release-calendar data.

High confidence

01 — Highest impact

What should happen next

The next beat should prove that MOUSE is being finished, not merely expanded. Ship the support patch, make the player-facing fixes legible on the store page, reactivate case-closed owners, then put weight behind Deluxe and Story DLC after the caveat language moves.

High confidence Strategic inference
01

Make mission replay the patch headline: 249 of 488 incomplete sampled players stopped at the cleanup cliff.

In the 500-player progression sample, 488 players had not completed all achievements; 249 of them stopped with "Tip of the Cheeseberg" as the latest observed unlock. The same sample shows 41.6% reaching 20 achievements, 10.6% reaching 25, and only 2.4% completing all 34. That is not generic retention softness; it is the main-case-to-cleanup handoff breaking.

If replay stays missing: completionists keep writing the clearest objections inside otherwise positive reviews, which makes their praise less useful as store-page proof.

06 — Timing

Patch before June's shooter pileup turns support into background noise.

The dates matter because each beat has a different audience job. Patch earns owner trust. Store refresh earns buyer clarity. Reactivation earns base activity. DLC earns monetization runway after the caveats thin out.

Directional signal Strategic inference
May 18-20

Primary patch window

PublisherIQ's May 18-24 window has 111 dated releases, 50 Mouse-related releases, and top overlaps including Luna Abyss, Blood High!, Reaper Actual, Beyond Enemy Lines - Vietnam, and Deep Rock Galactic: Rogue Core. It is crowded, but still cleaner than early June.

If the patch is thin here: the studio spends the best near-term window without changing the review caveat, so the June backup becomes damage control instead of momentum.

Jun 2-4

Backup patch window before SGF

The Jun 1-7 calendar is an avoid week: 313 dated releases, 145 Mouse-related releases, and direct/adjacent launches including Comatose, UNSIGHT, Blood Welkin, GNAW, and Kill Cast. Summer Game Fest runs June 5-8.

If support slips into SGF: the patch competes with reveals and preview coverage instead of owning the MOUSE conversation.

Jun 15-17

Reactivation window for case-closed owners

Jun 15-21 is the cleanest June opportunity: 32 dated releases, 9 Mouse-related releases, and ALARIC as the main named FPS overlap. Use this for mission replay, cleanup board, case log, or a short detective vignette.

If reactivation is skipped: August DLC has to rebuild owner attention from cold instead of speaking to players who recently came back to finish the case.

Aug 10-11

DLC roadmap window

PublisherIQ marks Aug 10-16 as recommended: 3 dated releases and 1 Mouse-related release in the window. Use it to clarify Story DLC timing, Deluxe entitlements, and the next-case promise before gamescom pressure later in August.

If caveats are still visible: keep the roadmap informational. Urgency should wait until owners have evidence that the base game has answered the repair list.

Avoid Jun 29-Jul 5 for support or DLC messaging. PublisherIQ's calendar shows 1,301 dated releases and 624 Mouse-related releases in that week, with Vivid Intrusion, PAYDAY: Aces High, WARAG, FEARMATES, and Ultra Damage among the top overlap names.
